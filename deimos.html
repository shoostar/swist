OVERVIEW

BROADSIDE DOMINATION

Ship number 813 'Deimos' is a Watcher-class battlecarrier capable of orbital subjugation. Built for Pyrrhus' Imperial Fleet in 2216, 'Deimos' first cut her teeth in victorious battle at the Fall of Masperon.

The most prominent feature of 'Deimos'' hull is its asymmetrical broadside design. Flanking the battlefield with its massive starboard shear, the 36 plasma cannons, 4 rocket pods, and a whopping 18 160mm artillery turrets absolutely shred anything in their firing line. While certainly not the most nimble of ships, the widened firing arc allows 'Deimos' to stay her course without having to correct its pitch or roll to compensate for poor firing position.

NO EXPENSE SPARED

Not a single armor plate, hangar rack, shitter tile, or even the most simple of cupholder will not be found made without but the finest of handcrafted combat steels this side of the Empyrion Galaxy. For a whopping quarter-million Iron ingots (amongst a grand shopping list of other items), you too can own your very own Harbinger of War. A disgustingly hot price to achieve God-mode, but well-worth it to the adventure capitalist.

Still have a heavy wallet after your purchase? Outfit your new battlecarrier with a fleet of up to 25 dock-specific SVs (more details on which ships I recommend coming soon). The open midship hangar measures 15m wide by 5m tall, with enough floor space to fit another 8 interceptors, or even some larger passenger transports and bombers. (For comparison, the Millenium Falcon SV found on the Steam Workshop will fit through the hangar openings, with room to spare!).

The enclosed hangars at fore have a capacity to hold 4 fighters on the top rack, and 3 bombers/interceptors in the bottom floor. In the rear, a small shuttle bay houses a salvage/recovery vessel for post-battle field operations. Under the bridge houses the last hangar, giving an effective berth of about 8.5m wide by 3m tall. Its landing platform extends beyond the extend of the armored hangar, allowing for two medium personnel transports to be docked abreast.

ANTI-NAVY ARMOR

Not to be outdone by its own armaments, 'Deimos' sports shielding on her broadside that reaches 8m thick at its most defensible points. Designed to withstand twice as much firepower as it can deliver, you'll be sure to have plenty of crewmembers—alive and well—for you to assign to refueling duties along the way to your next conquest. And trust us; you're going to need to.

While having a thick outer shell is always important, a strategically built interior is what really prevents your foes from truly crippling God's left hand. 1m thick flooring is standard throughout, while ample space between critical units allow for one-time destruction of relativaly small and contained areas. 'Deimos' was also engineered with redundancy in mind, so knocking out any of its primary support systems means your adversary has only scratched the surface. An intelligent captain will leverage the massive armor plateaus to secure a safe firing advantage over the battlefield.

Should you get yourself into a situation in which you have a low chance of survival, 'Deimos' comes equipped with 7 escape pods (5 fore, 2 aft) to ensure that you and your crew can get to safety. Each pod seats the pilot and 2 passengers, and includes a fridge, ammo storage, a mobile constructor, and plenty of storage. While not heavily armed, the hull is made entirely of hardened steel, ensuring structural integrity during your escape.

TRAVEL IN COMFORT

'Deimos' was built for the long-haul. As it hasn't nearly enough vertical thrust to remove itself from a planet's gravitational pull, the crew relies on this ship being completely self-sustaining. 20 modest but fashionably-decorated individual quarters provide safe and convenient living space for the ship's crew, while the captain enjoys an expansive personal lounge directly behind the main bridge.

Cargo space is in near-endless supply, as each of the two primary cargo hangars have a single-unit capacity of 192 crates. Additional storage can be found in the primary landing hangar, including 4 24-crate rooms directly below the hangar control deck. These storage rooms can serve as both public storage as well as ammunition/supply storage for the two fighter wings.

Below the aft engineering section lies a garden area split into two different levels, the lower level including the garden control center and passenger lounge, while the upper level has a dining area, with direct access to the kitchen.

Heading further aft, passengers will cross through the main O2 supply room before reaching the aft passenger lounge. Here, passengers can enjoy all the amenities of home with a big-screen TV, vending, seating, and an emergency O2 station.

WORK ORDERS

Throughout the ship, more defense positions are being devised. To the forward area of the main War Command Center, a highly-armored security room is being added, which will contain the ship's security control center, as well as an armory for security and away-team personnel. To deter intruders and prevent quick defeats in combat, 'Deimos' has had its core strategically placed behind very thick walls of armor in a highly obscure yet still accessible section of the keel.
