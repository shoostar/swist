PIF–813 DEIMOS

BROADSIDE DOMINATION

Ship number 813 'Deimos' is a Watcher-class battlecarrier capable of orbital subjugation. Built for Pyrrhus' Imperial Fleet in 2216, 'Deimos' first cut her teeth in victorious battle at the Fall of Masperon.

The most prominent feature of 'Deimos'' hull is its asymmetrical broadside design. Flanking a ship with its massive starboard shear, the 36 plasma cannons, 4 rocket pods, 4 flak cannons, and a whopping 18 160mm artillery turrets absolutely shred anything in their line of sight. While certainly not the most nimble of ships, the widened firing arc allows 'Deimos' to stay her course without having to correct its pitch or roll to compensate for poor firing position.

NO EXPENSE SPARED

It's expensive. Not a single armor plate, hangar rack, shitter tile, or even the most simple of cupholder will not be found made without but the finest of handcrafted combat steels this side of the Empyrion Galaxy. For a whopping quarter-million Iron ingots (amongst a grand shopping list of other items), you too can own your very own harbinger of war. Got a heavy wallet? This battlecarrier can hold a fleet of up to 18 area- and ship-specific SVs (more details on which ships I recommend). The midship open hangar measures 15m wide by 5m tall, with enough floor space to fit another 8 fighters, or even some larger passenger transports and bombers. (For comparison, the Millenium Falcon Mk.5 found on the Steam Workshop will fit through the hangar openings, with room to spare!).

The enclosed hangars at fore have a capacity to hold 4 fighters on the top rack, and 3 bombers/interceptors in the bottom floor. In the rear, a small shuttle bay houses small expeditionary vessels as scouts, away teams, recon ships, etc. Under the bridge houses the last ship, giving an effective berth of about 8.5m wide by 3m tall. Its landing platform extends beyond the extend of the armored hangar, allowing for two medium personnel transports to be docked abreast.

TRAVEL IN COMFORT

'Deimos' was built for the long-haul. As it hasn't nearly enough vertical thrust to remove itself from a planet's graviational pull, the crew relies on this ship being completely self-sustaining. 19 modest but fashionably-decorated individual quarters provide safe and convenient living space for the ship's crew, while the captain enjoys an expansive personal lounge directly behind the main bridge.

Cargo space in near-endless supply, as each of the two primary cargo hangars have a single-unit capacity of 96 crates. Additional storage can be found in the primary landing hangar, including 4 45-crate rooms directly below the hangar control deck. These storage rooms can serve as both public storage as well as ammunition/supply storage for the two fighter wings.
