PIF–813 DEIMOS

BROADSIDE DOMINATION

Ship number 813 'Deimos' is a Watcher-class battlecarrier capable of orbital subjugation. Built for Pyrrhus' Imperial Fleet in 2216, 'Deimos' first cut her teeth in victorious battle at the Fall of Masperon.

The most prominent feature of 'Deimos'' hull is its asymmetrical broadside design. Flanking a ship with its massive starboard shear, the 36 plasma cannons, 4 rocket pods, 4 flak cannons, and a whopping 18 160mm artillery turrets absolutely shred anything in their line of sight. While certainly not the most nimble of ships, the widened firing arc allows 'Deimos' to stay her course without having to correct its pitch or roll to compensate for poor firing position.

NO EXPENSE SPARED

It's expensive. Not a single armor plate, hangar rack, shitter tile, or even the most simple of cupholder will not be found made without but the finest of handcrafted combat steels this side of the Empyrion Galaxy. For a whopping quarter-million Iron ingots (amongst a grand shopping list of other items), you too can own your very own harbinger of war.

Still got a heavy wallet? Outfit this battlecarrier with a fleet of up to 25 dock-specific SVs (more details on which ships I recommend coming soon). The open midship hangar measures 15m wide by 5m tall, with enough floor space to fit another 8 interceptors, or even some larger passenger transports and bombers. (For comparison, the Millenium Falcon SV found on the Steam Workshop will fit through the hangar openings, with room to spare!).

The enclosed hangars at fore have a capacity to hold 4 fighters on the top rack, and 3 bombers/interceptors in the bottom floor. In the rear, a small shuttle bay houses small expeditionary vessels as scouts, away teams, recon ships, etc. Under the bridge houses the last ship, giving an effective berth of about 8.5m wide by 3m tall. Its landing platform extends beyond the extend of the armored hangar, allowing for two medium personnel transports to be docked abreast.

TRAVEL IN COMFORT

'Deimos' was built for the long-haul. As it hasn't nearly enough vertical thrust to remove itself from a planet's gravitational pull, the crew relies on this ship being completely self-sustaining. 19 modest but fashionably-decorated individual quarters provide safe and convenient living space for the ship's crew, while the captain enjoys an expansive personal lounge directly behind the main bridge.

Cargo space is in near-endless supply, as each of the two primary cargo hangars have a single-unit capacity of 96 crates. Additional storage can be found in the primary landing hangar, including 4 45-crate rooms directly below the hangar control deck. These storage rooms can serve as both public storage as well as ammunition/supply storage for the two fighter wings.

CURRENT SHIPYARD WORK ORDERS

The bow/port-bow section is currently being finished. This is to include the 4th fuel storage room, forward gravity generators, tertiary oxygen and ammo supplies, a secondary command deck (above the forward fighter hangars), and additional primary system redundancy. Reverse thrusters are already in place on the front of the vessel, while plans to add radar/scanning hubs, forward defenses, and escape pod breaches are being drawn.

To the aft, the lower engineering decks are currently being re-designed. A fully-armored ammo hold, generator rooms, and system integrations with the ship's warp core have already been constructed, while the primary oxygen depot and vegetation farm are on the way.

Throughout the ship, more defense positions are being devised. To the forward area of the main War Command Center, a highly-armored security room is being added, which will contain the ship's security control center, as well as an armory for security and away-team personnel. To deter intruders and prevent quick defeats in combat, 'Deimos' has had its core strategically placed behind very thick walls of armor in a highly obscure yet still accessible section of the keel.
